Bezos is Richer Than You (Unfortunately)

With a net worth of $201 billion, Jeff Bezos is one of the richest people in the world, second to Elon Musk.

The following assets make up Bezos' fortune:

Amazon ownership stake of 11% ($173 billion)

Blue Origin ($9.15 billion)

The Washington Post ($250 million)

$16.1 billion in cash

Bezos stepped down as CEO of Amazon because he wanted to "focus on his other companies," which are:

The Washington Post

Blue Origin

Bezos Expeditions

Bezos Day One Fund

He also has investments elsewhere.

Business Insider did the math to calculate how much money Jeff Bezos makes in a year, month, week, day, hour, minute, and second.

They even made this infographic.

Breaking it down, Bezos made:

$78.5 billion in a year, $6.54 billion a month, $1.51 billion a week, $215 million a day, $9 million per hour, $150 thousand a minute, and $2.5 thousand a second.

Putting things into perspective:

Bezos' earnings per hour are 315 times Amazon workers' median annual pay of $28,466. To earn what Bezos makes in one hour, an Amazon worker earning the $15 minimum wage would have to work about 597,412 hours (24 hours a day for the next 68 years).

In one minute, Bezos makes more than three times what the median US worker makes in a year, which is $45,552.

Bezos' earnings per second are more than twice what the median US worker makes in one week, which is $876.

This article was last updated in January 2019, so Bezos' earnings are much higher now.

Bezos has a salary!

Jeff has received a base salary of $81,840 since 1998, which is still more than the median household income in the US ($68,703 in 2019). In 2020, he took no bonuses, stocks, or options, but received about $1.6 million in additional compensation.

If you break it down, Bezos' annual income of $1.7 million turns out to be $140 thousand per month, $32 thousand per week, $4 thousand per day, $200 per hour, or $3.20 per minute.

With Bezos' net worth, you would expect him to be more generous, right? WRONG.

  • That time he donated $500,000 to charity.

    At a gala for the Baby 2 Baby charity, Bezos donated $500,000. This was right after someone had donated $1 million. The $500,000 Bezos donated represents 0.000001% of his net worth. The median American household has a net worth of $121,411. If you donated 0.000001% of that, it would be 30 cents...

  • Bezos' "generous" contribution to Feeding America.

    In April 2020, Bezos donated $100 million to Feeding America, which is the largest hunger-relief organization in the United States. Bezos' contribution was the largest gift in the history of the organization. At the time, $100 million was .07% of Bezos' fortune, which is the equivalent to 7 cents out of a $100 bill.

  • When he committed $10 billion to address climate change.

    In 2020, Bezos said he was committing $10 billion to address the climate crisis through an initiative he called the Bezos Earth Fund. Bezos said (through an Instagram post), "Climate change is the biggest threat to our planet. I want to work alongside others both to amplify known ways and to explore new ways of fighting the devastating impact of climate change on this planet we all share." Amazon is worth over $1 trillion and is one of the world's top emitters, emitting the equivalent of 60.64 million metric tons of carbon dioxide in 2020, a 19% increase from 51.17 million metric tons in 2019.
