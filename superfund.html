Amazon Made More Money During the Pandemic

  • Amazon's pandemic profits top previous three years of earnings.

    Amazon earned a net income of $26.9 billion between March 2020 and March 2021.

    Between 2017 and 2019, Amazon had a total net income of $24.7 billion.

  • Amazon's profits tripled in first quarter.

    Amazon reported $108.5 billion in sales in the first three months of this year, an increase of 44% compared to last year.

  • Amazon (and Walmart) could have quadrupled the Covid-19 compensation to workers and still earned more profit than last year.

    Amazon and Walmart have earned an extra $10.7 billion over 2019's profits because of the pandemic.

    Stock prices for Amazon and Walmart increased by 70% and 36%, while worker wages have only increased by 7% and 6%.

    Bezos earned $75.6 billion in 2020, 42 times the hazard pay that about 1 million workers received ($1.8 billion).

  • Amazon raised prices on essential items.

    In the months after the pandemic began, Amazon charged inflated prices for essentials such as disposable gloves, soap, face masks, and hand sanitizers.
